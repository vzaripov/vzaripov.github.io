About

Hi! My name is Vadim Zaripov. I study theoretical computer science, with interests in complexity theory, derandomization, spectral graph theory, and coding theory.

I recently completed my B.A./M.S. degree at Harvard. My undergraduate thesis, advised by Prof. Salil Vadhan, was focused on exploring combinatorial properties of bounded-independence subsampling of edges in graphs. If you have any questions about my research or would like to collaborate, you can reach me with: vadim.r.zaripov at gmail.com.
